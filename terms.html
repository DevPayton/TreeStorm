What the service does

TreeStorm Leads monitors public National Weather Service alerts and notifies you, by SMS and/or email, when qualifying severe-weather events affect the ZIP codes in your territory. Our alerts rank affected ZIP codes by estimated tree-damage risk, using factors including wind speed and recent rainfall (soil saturation).

Our alerts are informational tools to help you prioritize outreach. They identify areas where storm conditions occurred — they do not confirm that any specific property has damage, and they do not guarantee that any job, lead, or revenue will result. Tree-damage risk scoring is an estimate, not a measurement of actual damage.

Your territory and exclusivity

Your plan includes a set number of ZIP codes ("your territory"), based on the tier you choose. ZIP codes are exclusive: while you hold a ZIP code, no other TreeStorm Leads subscriber can claim it. You select your ZIP codes in your dashboard, subject to availability and the radius limits of your plan.

What happens to your ZIPs if you cancel: when your subscription ends, the ZIP codes you held are released and become available for other contractors to claim. We cannot guarantee a ZIP code will still be available if you later resubscribe.
